Wind Speed

Northern Hemisphere more windier than the Southern Hemisphere

W   ind Speed is a fundamental atmosphere quantity caused by air moving from high to low pressure
Wind Speed is affected by a number of factors like pressure gradient, Jet streams, Rossby waves and local weather conditions
This data was collected on 22nd July which corresponds to peak summer in the Northern Hemisphere.
During the Northern Hemisphere summer, easterly jets can form in tropical regions, typically where dry air encounters more humid air at high altitudes.
As we can see in the figure, highest wind speed is observed in the Northern Hemisphere.
Summer season is also the onset of hurricanes and atmospheric disturbances which result in higher wind speeds in the Northern Hemisphere.
